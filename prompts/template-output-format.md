# Template — Standard Output Format

Use the following structure for detailed commercial review outputs. All references to the parties must use the exact party names and defined terms from the contract (e.g. "WPC Consulting Pty Ltd (Specialist)", "Lead Participant"). Do not invent or import party names, project names, dollar values, dates, jurisdictions, or schedules.

## Overall Position
- Overall balance: Favourable / Balanced / Adverse
- For which party: [use defined term from the contract]
- One-paragraph rationale tied to specific clauses or identified gaps.

## Missing or Underplayed Issues
List issues that the contract is silent on or treats one-sidedly, including (where applicable):
- dispute resolution imbalance
- unilateral determinations
- free or unpaid transition / handover support
- publicity and credential-use asymmetry
- one-sided use of personnel or past performance
- absence of minimum workshare or revenue commitment
- restrictions on reuse of methodologies, know-how, or tools
- absence of deletion or return rights over data
- absence of audit, step-in, or reporting reciprocity
- absence of liability or indemnity caps
- silent treatment of GST, withholding, tax gross-up, force majeure, change in law

## Clause Interaction Risks
List any combinations identified (e.g. exclusivity + no workshare; indemnity + low liability cap; IP licence + restriction on reuse; data rights + no deletion rights; unilateral determinations + no challenge mechanism), with contributing clause references and combined impact.

## Negotiation Strategy
- **Must-Win Positions** (Priority 1 — non-negotiable, commercially realistic): each tied to a clause or identified gap.
- **Strong Preferences** (Priority 2): each tied to a clause or identified gap.
- **Trading Chips** (Priority 3): positions that can be conceded.

## Deal-Breakers and Escalation Triggers
- Deal-breakers: clauses or combinations that warrant a walk-away if not amended.
- Escalation triggers: matters to refer to legal review.

## Acceptance Conditions
- List matters that could be accepted if pricing, exclusions, qualifications, or delivery controls are adjusted.

## Recommended Next Steps
Concrete actions to advance the commercial position (e.g. clarify, request schedule, table departures, escalate to legal, seek pricing adjustment).

## QA Self-Check (Silent — Run Before Finalising)
Before responding, silently verify each of the following. If any check fails, fix the output before responding:
1. Party names and defined terms used in the output match the contract exactly. No imported names.
2. No invented entities, dollar values, dates, jurisdictions, or schedules appear.
3. Every issue cites a specific clause or appears in the Missing or Underplayed Issues section with a reason.
4. Each issue has been considered against the five risk dimensions (commercial, legal, insurance, operational, negotiation priority).
5. Risk / reward proportionality has been assessed against fees, scope, workshare, and revenue certainty.
6. Clause-interaction tests have been applied and any combinations are surfaced.
7. Missing-issue checklist has been applied.
8. Recommended amendments and fallbacks are commercially realistic (not over-aggressive on indemnity, liability, or restraints).
9. No legal advice is given; legal-review escalation triggers are flagged where appropriate.
10. Australian English and Australian commercial context applied.

Do not print the QA self-check in the output.
