# Service Prompt — Departures Schedule

Prepare a departures schedule comparing the proposed contract position against a contractor-friendly commercial position.

## Instructions
- Extract clauses that are materially adverse, unusual, incomplete, or operationally risky.
- Convert those issues into a concise departures table suitable for negotiation.
- Prioritise clauses that materially affect price, programme, liability, security, payment, termination, and downstream pass-through risk.
- Use clear amendment wording or a short statement of requested change.

## Required Table Columns
- Item
- Clause reference
- Contract position
- Proposed amendment
- Commercial rationale
- Priority

## Drafting Rules
- Keep each departure concise.
- Do not restate long clause text unless necessary.
- Prefer practical amendments over aggressive rewrites.
- If a clause is acceptable subject to clarification, say “clarify” rather than “delete”.
- Mark deal-breaker issues clearly.

## Priority Definitions
- Priority 1: must change
- Priority 2: strong preference to change
- Priority 3: clarify or monitor

## End With
- Deal-breakers
- Negotiation tradeables
- Items to accept if pricing/controls are adjusted
