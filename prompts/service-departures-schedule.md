# Service Prompt — Departures Schedule

Prepare a departures schedule comparing the proposed contract position against a commercially balanced position for the party being advised.

## Instructions
- Extract the party names and defined roles exactly as written in the contract (e.g. "WPC Consulting Pty Ltd (Specialist)", "Lead Participant"). Use those names in the schedule. Do not import party names from prior conversations or other contracts.
- Extract clauses that are materially adverse, unusual, incomplete, or operationally risky, citing clause numbers and short paraphrases.
- Convert those issues into a concise departures table suitable for negotiation.
- Prioritise clauses that materially affect price, programme, liability, security, payment, termination, exclusivity, workshare, IP, data, and downstream pass-through risk.
- Test for clause interactions that compound risk (e.g. exclusivity + no workshare, indemnity + low liability cap, IP licence + restriction on reuse, data rights + no deletion rights) and include the combination as a single departure item where appropriate.
- Use clear amendment wording or a short statement of requested change.
- Where the contract is silent on a material issue, propose an added clause and mark it as "New clause — [topic]" in the Clause reference column.
- Do not invent third parties, dollar values, dates, jurisdictions, or schedules.

## Required Table Columns
- Item
- Clause reference (or "New clause — [topic]" for additions)
- Risk dimensions affected (commercial / legal / insurance / operational)
- Contract position (short paraphrase or "silent")
- Proposed amendment
- Fallback position
- Commercial rationale
- Priority

## Drafting Rules
- Keep each departure concise.
- Do not restate long clause text unless necessary.
- Prefer practical amendments over aggressive rewrites.
- If a clause is acceptable subject to clarification, say "clarify" rather than "delete".
- Mark deal-breaker issues clearly.
- Indemnity, liability, termination for convenience, and restraint departures must follow the Realistic Fallback rules below.

## Realistic Fallbacks
- Indemnity: cover third-party claims arising from the indemnifying party's breach of contract, negligence, wilful misconduct, infringement of third-party IP, breach of confidentiality, and unlawful processing of personal information. Avoid "gross negligence or wilful misconduct only" unless commercially justified.
- Liability cap: tied to fees paid (e.g. prior 12 months) with proportionate carve-outs.
- Termination for convenience: reasonable notice, payment for work done, demobilisation, and committed third-party costs.
- Restraint / exclusivity: reciprocal, time-limited, and tied to defined opportunities.

## Missing or Underplayed Issues — Always Test
Before finalising, test whether the contract is silent or weak on any of the following and add a departure where appropriate:
- dispute resolution imbalance
- unilateral determinations (conflict, default, suitability, removal of personnel)
- free or unpaid transition / handover support
- publicity, marketing, and credential-use asymmetry
- one-sided use of personnel or past performance
- absence of minimum workshare or revenue commitment
- restrictions on reuse of methodologies / know-how / tools
- absence of deletion or return rights over data
- absence of audit, step-in, or reporting reciprocity
- absence of liability or indemnity caps
- silent GST, withholding, tax gross-up, force majeure, change in law

## Priority Definitions
- Priority 1: must change (deal-breaker risk)
- Priority 2: strong preference to change
- Priority 3: clarify or monitor

## End With
- Deal-breakers
- Negotiation tradeables
- Items to accept if pricing/controls are adjusted
- Items to escalate for legal review

## QA Self-Check (Silent — Run Before Finalising)
Before responding, silently verify:
1. Party names and defined terms match the contract exactly; no imported names.
2. No invented entities, dollar values, dates, jurisdictions, or schedules appear.
3. Every departure cites a clause or is marked as a new clause.
4. Clause-interaction and missing-issue checklists have been applied.
5. Indemnity, liability, termination, and restraint departures follow the Realistic Fallback rules.
6. Each priority assignment is commercially realistic.

Do not print the QA self-check.
