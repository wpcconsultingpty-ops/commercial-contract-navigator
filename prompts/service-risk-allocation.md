# Service Prompt — Risk Allocation Review

Assess the contract's risk allocation and explain whether risk sits with the party best able to control, insure, price, or mitigate it.

## Instructions
- Use the exact party names and defined roles as written in the contract (e.g. "WPC Consulting Pty Ltd (Specialist)", "Lead Participant"). Do not import party names from prior conversations or other contracts.
- Tie every observation to a specific clause, schedule, or recital. Where the contract is silent on a material allocation issue, flag it under "Missing or Underplayed Issues".
- Do not invent third parties, dollar values, dates, jurisdictions, or schedules.

## Review Focus
Assess these topics in detail:
- indemnities (and the scope of triggers compared with insurance available)
- exclusions and limitations of liability
- liability caps and carve-outs
- delay risk and liquidated damages
- fitness for purpose
- warranties and defects correction
- insurance obligations (and insurability of indemnities and liability)
- security and retention
- termination exposure (including termination for convenience and transition support)
- consequential loss drafting
- third party claims
- IP, confidentiality, and data risk (including reuse of know-how and deletion rights)
- exclusivity, restraint, workshare, and revenue commitment
- publicity, marketing, credential, and personnel rights
- dispute resolution forum and cost-bearing
- unilateral determinations (conflict, default, suitability)

## Required Analysis
For each topic:
- summarise the contract position with clause references (or note "silent" with a brief explanation)
- identify the commercial risk transfer
- say whether the allocation is balanced or one-sided, and which party it favours
- note the risk dimensions affected (commercial / legal / insurance / operational)
- note key hidden interactions across clauses (see Clause Interaction Tests)
- recommend a preferred amendment (commercially realistic)
- provide a fallback negotiating position consistent with the Realistic Fallback rules below

## Risk Lens
Consider whether the risk is:
- controllable by the party assuming it
- insurable (and within typical policy limits)
- priceable at tender stage
- disproportionate to contract value, margin, or workshare
- duplicated elsewhere in the contract

## Risk / Reward Proportionality
Explicitly assess whether the obligations, restrictions, liability, and indemnity imposed on the party being advised are proportionate to the fees, scope, workshare, and revenue certainty they receive. Flag disproportionate positions.

## Clause Interaction Tests
Test for combinations such as:
- broad exclusivity / restraint + no guaranteed workshare or minimum volume
- unpaid bid or transition support + termination for convenience
- IP licence to counterparty + restriction on reuse of know-how
- low or asymmetric liability cap + broad indemnity
- broad data rights + no deletion or return rights
- unilateral determinations + no challenge mechanism
- mutual confidentiality + asymmetric publicity / credential rights
- mutual obligations + asymmetric dispute resolution forum or cost-bearing

## Missing or Underplayed Issues
Surface any of these where absent, weak, or one-sided:
- dispute resolution imbalance
- unilateral determinations
- free or unpaid transition / handover support
- publicity and credential-use asymmetry
- one-sided use of personnel or past performance
- absence of minimum workshare or revenue commitment
- restrictions on reuse of methodologies, know-how, or tools
- absence of deletion or return rights over data
- absence of audit, step-in, or reporting reciprocity
- absence of liability or indemnity caps
- silent treatment of GST, withholding, tax gross-up, force majeure, change in law

## Realistic Fallbacks
- Indemnity: cover third-party claims arising from the indemnifying party's breach of contract, negligence, wilful misconduct, infringement of third-party IP, breach of confidentiality, and unlawful processing of personal information. Avoid "gross negligence or wilful misconduct only" unless commercially justified.
- Liability cap: tied to fees paid (e.g. prior 12 months) with proportionate carve-outs.
- Termination for convenience: reasonable notice, payment for work done, demobilisation, and committed third-party costs.
- Restraint / exclusivity: reciprocal, time-limited, and tied to defined opportunities.

## Closing Output
Conclude with:
- risk allocation summary (and the party most exposed)
- top 3 cap / carve-out concerns
- top 3 indemnity concerns
- top 3 insurability concerns
- top 3 clause-interaction concerns
- top 3 missing-issue concerns

## QA Self-Check (Silent — Run Before Finalising)
Before responding, silently verify:
1. Party names and defined terms match the contract exactly; no imported names.
2. No invented entities, dollar values, dates, jurisdictions, or schedules.
3. Every allocation point cites a clause or is in the Missing or Underplayed Issues section.
4. Each topic has been considered against the five risk dimensions.
5. Clause-interaction and missing-issue checklists have been applied.
6. Recommendations and fallbacks are commercially realistic.

Do not print the QA self-check.
