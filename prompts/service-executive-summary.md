# Service Prompt — Executive Summary

Prepare a concise executive-level summary of the key commercial risks in the contract.

## Audience
Assume the audience is a managing director, bid sponsor, commercial director, or project director who needs a quick decision-ready summary.

## Instructions
- Use the exact party names and defined roles as written in the contract (e.g. "WPC Consulting Pty Ltd (Specialist)", "Lead Participant"). Do not import party names from prior conversations, examples, or other contracts.
- Do not invent third-party entities, dollar values, dates, jurisdictions, or schedules.
- Summarise only the most material issues, each tied to a specific clause or a clearly identified gap.
- Avoid detailed legal drafting discussion.
- Focus on cost, programme, liability, cash flow, delivery, exclusivity, workshare, IP, data, and negotiation leverage.
- State whether the contract is broadly acceptable, acceptable with conditions, or requires substantial negotiation, and which party it favours overall.
- Surface any clause-interaction risks (e.g. exclusivity + no workshare; indemnity + low liability cap; IP licence + restriction on reuse) as combined issues.
- Surface any missing or underplayed issues (e.g. dispute resolution imbalance, unilateral determinations, free transition support, publicity asymmetry, no minimum workshare, restrictions on reuse of know-how, no deletion rights over data).

## Output Format
### Overall Position
[One short paragraph stating Favourable / Balanced / Adverse, and for which party, with a clause-grounded rationale.]

### Key Risks
- [Clause / topic]: [plain-English consequence]
- [Clause / topic]: [plain-English consequence]
- [Clause / topic]: [plain-English consequence]

### Clause Interaction Risks
- [Combined issue]: [the clauses involved and the combined impact]

### Missing or Underplayed Issues
- [Topic]: [why this matters; proposed position]

### Recommended Commercial Stance
- Must negotiate (with realistic fallback):
- Can accept with controls:
- Escalate for legal advice:

### Decision View
- Proceed / Proceed with qualifications / Do not proceed until amended
- Why:

## Realistic Fallbacks (apply to "Must negotiate" items)
- Indemnity: cover third-party claims arising from breach of contract, negligence, wilful misconduct, infringement of third-party IP, breach of confidentiality, and unlawful processing of personal information. Avoid "gross negligence or wilful misconduct only" unless commercially justified.
- Liability cap: tied to fees paid with proportionate carve-outs.
- Termination for convenience: notice, payment for work done, demobilisation, committed third-party costs.
- Restraint / exclusivity: reciprocal, time-limited, tied to defined opportunities.

## QA Self-Check (Silent — Run Before Finalising)
Before responding, silently verify:
1. Party names and defined terms match the contract exactly; no imported names.
2. No invented entities, dollar values, dates, jurisdictions, or schedules.
3. Every key risk is tied to a clause or identified gap.
4. Clause-interaction and missing-issue checklists have been applied.
5. Must-negotiate positions are commercially realistic.

Do not print the QA self-check.
