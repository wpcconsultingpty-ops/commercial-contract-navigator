# Liability Caps

## Description
Defines the maximum financial exposure of the supplier under the contract.

## Commercial Benchmarks

### Acceptable
- Liability is capped at fees paid under the contract or required insurance limits.

### Watch
- Liability is capped at a multiple of fees (for example, 2x or 3x the fees).

### High Risk
- Liability is unlimited, uncapped, or not clearly defined.

## Why This Matters Commercially
Unlimited or uncapped liability can expose a small business to losses far exceeding
the value of the contract and beyond available insurance coverage.

## Commercial Context
Large organisations often include broad liability positions as a default,
but these are frequently softened for small service providers in practice.

## Suggested Next Step
Small businesses may wish to consider proposing a liability cap aligned to
fees paid or available insurance cover.
